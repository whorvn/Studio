Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers.

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a collaborative event where programmers, designers, and other tech enthusiasts come together to build innovative solutions within a limited timeframe, typically 24-48 hours. Participants work in teams to solve challenges, create projects, and compete for prizes.

Do I need to have a team to participate?

No, you can register for hackathons individually and use HackConnect's team formation feature to find teammates with complementary skills. You can also participate as a solo competitor if the hackathon allows it.

What skill level do I need to join a hackathon?

Hackathons are for everyone! There are events appropriate for all skill levels, from beginners to experienced professionals. Many hackathons specifically welcome newcomers and provide mentors to help you along the way.

How do I book a mentor session?

Once you've created an account on HackConnect, navigate to the Mentors section where you can browse available mentors, view their expertise and schedule, and book a session that fits your needs. Some sessions may be free, while others may require HackConnect credits.

Are hackathons only for coders?

Not at all! Hackathons need diverse skills including design, project management, domain expertise, and business acumen. The best teams often combine members with different backgrounds and abilities.

How do I win a hackathon?

Success in hackathons typically comes from a combination of innovation, good execution, and effective presentation. Focus on solving a real problem, create a working prototype, and make sure you can clearly explain your solution's value and technical implementation.
